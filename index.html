What is Ohm's Law?

Ohm's Law is the foundation of electrical engineering, establishing the linear relationship between Voltage (V), Current (I), and Resistance (R). Discovered by Georg Simon Ohm, it states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across them.

Common Applications

Engineers use this daily for circuit design, determining the correct resistor for an LED, and calculating the power consumption of household appliances. It is essential for analyzing both DC and AC circuits in electrical prototyping.

Understanding Voltage Drop

Voltage drop refers to the reduction in potential difference as electric current moves through the passive elements (wires) of an electrical circuit. High resistance in long cables leads to energy loss as heat, reducing the voltage available to the load.

Cable Sizing & Safety

Calculating voltage drop is crucial for electrical installations to comply with safety standards (like DIN VDE). It ensures that motors and electronics receive sufficient power. Key factors include conductor length, cross-sectional area, and material conductivity (κ).
